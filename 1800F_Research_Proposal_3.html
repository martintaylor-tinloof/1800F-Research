01 Overview

Programme scope

This phase covers three areas of research: the financial and competitive landscape, what consumers feel and want from flower gifting, and the cultural conditions currently shaping the category.

The output is a single research document in four parts, providing the evidence base needed before brand strategy and execution begin. All three tracks run in parallel across eight weeks, starting from a brief alignment session to confirm scope, access, and methodology.

02 Methodology

Research framework

Three tracks run in parallel — market intelligence, consumer truth, and cultural signals — converging into a single output document.

Track 1 — Market & Financial Intelligence

The financial and competitive picture

Deep secondary research working from primary sources: SEC filings and Form 8-Ks, earnings call transcripts, and analyst reports from the past two years. Produces a verified picture of the revenue trajectory, margin structure, and promotional dependency. The competitive analysis maps positioning, pricing, and occasion targeting across the primary players in flower and gifting.

Key questions
  • What is the revenue decline rate, and where in the P&L is it most acute?
  • Which competitors are gaining ground — and what positioning gap does that reveal?
  • What have comparable purpose-led repositionings achieved, and over what timeframe?

Outputs

  • Financial trajectory analysis with source-verified data
  • Competitive positioning map with white space assessment
  • KPI baseline inputs and ROI model inputs
Track 2 — Consumer & Brand Truth

What people feel about flower gifting

Primary qualitative research using laddering interviews — a structured depth method based on means-end chain theory. The interviewer starts with a specific, recent gifting decision and probes upward (why does that matter to you?) until the respondent reaches a value they cannot further abstract. The method surfaces motivation rather than stated preference, and is more reliable than surveys or focus groups as a result. This track covers both the emotional experience of giving flowers and how consumers currently perceive the brand and its products.

Key questions
  • What are people ultimately trying to feel when they give flowers?
  • How does the product experience — quality on arrival, brand trust — factor into gifting confidence?
  • Is there an unmet need for a platform that prioritises giving right over giving fast?

Outputs

  • Means-end chain maps by segment and terminal value hierarchy
  • Brand and product perception findings
  • Verbatim quotes by segment for use in the brand strategy phase
Track 3 — Cultural & Expert Signals

What the culture is doing

Secondary research into the documented shifts reshaping flower gifting — changes in occasion structure, social contexts, and what academic and institutional research says about the role of gifting across relationships and demographics. Adjacent category analysis covers brands that have repositioned around emotional connection, establishing proof of mechanism. Supported by four to six structured expert conversations: researchers in consumer gifting psychology, cultural strategists, and brand practitioners with direct experience in purpose-led repositionings.

Key questions
  • How is the social function of flower gifting changing, and what is driving it?
  • Is the cultural window for this repositioning currently open — and what would close it?
  • What do adjacent category repositionings tell us about what the mechanism actually requires?

Outputs

  • Cultural gifting shift analysis with sources
  • Adjacent category case studies — five to seven examples with documented outcomes
  • Expert synthesis: integrated findings from four to six domain specialists
03 Output

Research document

A single comprehensive document in four parts. Each part is written to work as a standalone read.

Part I
Evidence Synthesis
Full synthesis of all three research tracks with all sources cited. The master source and foundation for brand strategy in the next phase.
Part II
Board Brief
A condensed version of the evidence synthesis for senior decision-makers. Leads with the financial case, supports with consumer and cultural evidence. No methodology, no footnotes. Maximum eight pages.
Part III
KPI Baseline & Measurement Framework
Five measurement pillars established and baselined. Current-state readings, Year 1 targets, Year 3 targets. Built so the performance of the repositioning is measurable from the first day it goes live.
Part IV
ROI Inputs & Financial Case
Verified inputs for an ROI model: decline rate, category benchmarks, comparable repositioning outcomes, and lever assumptions from the research. The model is built by the finance team from these inputs, grounding the financial argument in 1-800-Flowers’ own data.
04 Timeline

Eight weeks, four phases

Each phase closes with an internal review before the next opens.

Phase 1 — Foundations (weeks 1–2)

Brief alignment to confirm scope, access, and methodology. Participant recruitment placed. Data access established. Desk research framework built.

Phase 2 — Secondary research (weeks 3–4)

Track 1 in full production. Track 3 secondary underway. Expert interviewees identified and scheduled. Laddering interview guide written and reviewed.

Phase 3 — Primary research (weeks 5–6)

Laddering sessions across ten working days. Expert conversations in the same window. Means-end chains mapped in real time as sessions complete.

Phase 4 — Synthesis and delivery (weeks 7–8)

Full synthesis across all three tracks. KPI framework baselined. ROI inputs assembled. All four document parts produced, reviewed, and delivered.
